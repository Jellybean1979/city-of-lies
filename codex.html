Field Operations Manual — Confidential How to Play City of Lies
The Premise

You are a detective in a city that has learned to lie to itself. A case lands on your desk — straightforward on the surface, rotten underneath. The deeper you dig, the more you'll find that the city's institutions are compromised, its people are scared, and the truth costs more than most are willing to pay.

You decide how far you're willing to go to close the case.

Navigation More coming soon

Your Investigation Board is the hub of every case. From there, you can access your Evidence Locker, Case Files, Suspect Profiles, and your Field Journal. Everything you discover gets logged automatically.

Use the Audio Lab to analyse intercepted recordings and ambient sound evidence. The Final Report is where you submit your conclusions — but you can only submit once, so be certain.

Gathering Evidence More coming soon

Evidence is collected by interacting with locations, suspects, and contacts during active investigation scenes. Items found in the field are automatically tagged with location, time, and chain-of-custody details and added to your Evidence Locker.

Detective's Note Not all evidence is found in obvious places. Pay attention to background details, overheard conversations, and what suspects don't say. The city has layers.
The Three Meters More coming soon

Your choices continuously affect three city-wide measurements. Influence Level tracks your personal corruption. City Corruption measures the health of the city's institutions. Street Credibility reflects your standing in the criminal underworld.

These meters shape what options are available to you, which contacts will talk, and which of the game's multiple endings you'll reach. There is no single "good" path — every approach closes some doors and opens others.

Factions More coming soon

The city is divided into six factions, each with their own agendas, contacts, and information. Building relationships with factions unlocks new investigative avenues, but earning one faction's trust often comes at the cost of another's.

Closing a Case More coming soon

When you believe you have enough evidence, you can file your Final Report and name a perpetrator. The outcome depends on the quality of your evidence, your standing with key factions, and the state of your three meters at the time of submission.

You cannot unsign a final report. Make sure you're ready.

Warning Submitting a report that implicates powerful people without sufficient evidence will not end well for you. The city protects its own.
