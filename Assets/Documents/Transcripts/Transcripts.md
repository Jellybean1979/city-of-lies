VERSION B — Suspect Cracks (Partial Confession)
Interrogator: [calmly] Let's begin. Tell me where you were between 9 and 10 PM last night.
Suspect: [nervous] I… I already told you. I was walking home.
Interrogator: [skeptical] That's interesting, because the CCTV footage shows someone matching your description near the alley.
Suspect: [defensive] That wasn't me. You're seeing things.
Interrogator: [slides photograph across table] Look at it. Take your time.
Suspect: [long pause] ...That could be anyone.
Interrogator: [leans forward] Then explain why your phone pinged off the tower two blocks away.
Suspect: [swallows] I… I don't know. Maybe the signal bounced or something.
Interrogator: [firm] That's not how cell towers work. Try again.
Suspect: [voice cracks] I'm telling you the truth!
Interrogator: [quietly] We also found something at the scene. Something with your initials on it.
Suspect: [sharp inhale] [long silence] ...Okay. Okay. I was there. But I didn't do anything. I swear I didn't touch him.
Interrogator: [evenly] Then what were you doing there?
Suspect: [exhales shakily] I was supposed to deliver something. An envelope. Someone paid me to drop it at the alley entrance at 9:30 and walk away. That's all I did. I dropped it and I left.
Interrogator: Who paid you?
Suspect: [hesitates] I don't know his name. I never got a name. We communicated through a number — a burner phone, I think. He texted me the instructions.
Interrogator: What was in the envelope?
Suspect: [genuinely distressed] I don't know. I didn't open it. He said if I opened it I'd regret it. I believed him.
Interrogator: [leans back] And when did you realise something had happened in that alley?
Suspect: [voice drops] When I saw the news the next morning. I threw up. I swear I didn't know what I was walking into.
Interrogator: [quietly] You should have come to us sooner.
Suspect: [barely a whisper] I know. I was scared. I'm still scared.

Suspect: [coldly] No. We're not. I know how this works. You've got a photograph that proves nothing and a cell tower ping that any defence attorney will have thrown out in twenty minutes.
Interrogator: You seem to know a lot about how evidence works.
Suspect: [thin smile] I watch the news. I want a lawyer. Right now. This conversation is over.
Interrogator: [beat] That's your right.
Suspect: [leans forward] And when my lawyer gets here, we're going to have a very different conversation about how you people are harassing an innocent citizen. Write that down.
Interrogator: [stands, closes folder] Interview suspended. Subject requested legal counsel. [to suspect, quietly] You're making this harder for yourself.
Suspect: [says nothing, looks away]
