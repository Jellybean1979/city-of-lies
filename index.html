How to Play

"The truth is here. You just have to know where to look."

THE GOAL

A man is dead. Your job is to find out who killed him, where, and how — then decide what to do about it. Gather evidence, interrogate witnesses, and when you're ready, file your theory.

THE BOARD

The corkboard is your investigation hub. Every pinned note is a piece of evidence. Tap any note to open it. Green dots mean you've reviewed that piece. No dots means you haven't looked yet.

EVIDENCE TYPES

CCTV — Review footage. Look for timing, faces, vehicles, and anomalies.
AUDIO — Listen to recordings and examine spectrograms for hidden clues.
DOCUMENTS — Read reports, financials, and field notes carefully.
INTERROGATION — Question suspects and witnesses. Watch the lie detector.

SPECTROGRAMS

Audio spectrograms are visual representations of sound. Look for anomalies: sudden silences, unexpected frequency spikes, or patterns that don't match the stated content. Hidden clues are buried in the noise.

LIE DETECTOR

During interrogations, a simulated polygraph tracks heart rate, stress, and truthfulness. High stress and heart rate spikes don't always mean guilt — but they always mean something. No verbal response is as telling as any answer.

FILING YOUR THEORY

When you're ready: name the culprit, the location, and the method — like Clue. If the accused is powerful, a second choice appears. You can expose them to the DA — or take the money and bury it. Both paths lead somewhere. Neither is without consequence.

THE ENDING

There are four possible outcomes: the right verdict exposed, the right verdict buried, the wrong verdict, and a partial truth. The city doesn't forget. Neither does your conscience.

DECEASED

Last known location: Meridian Hotel charity gala, attending as press. Left the ballroom at an unknown time and was found dead in the adjacent parking structure.
